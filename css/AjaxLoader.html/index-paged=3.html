19th Amendment to the Constitution of Pakistan

June 16th, 2010




Soon, we will see 19th amendment in the constitution, something like this:



Article 62: Qualifications for membership of Parliament

A person shall not be qualified to be elected or chosen as a member of Parliament, unless:

a) He possesses a fake degree.

b) He is matriculation, or at most intermediate. Candidates with any further genuine qualification shall be regarded as ineligible. However, candidates with fake matriculation/intermediate certification shall remain eligible.

c) He has involvement in fraud cases. Candidate must exhibit court orders of the fraud case(s) upon request if required.

d) Incase candidate fail to meet the criteria in paragraph (c); he/she must possess previous records of imprisonment or exile.

e) He is convicted for immoral crimes and false giving

f) He should not bear any basic or advanced knowledge of Islamiat

g) He has records of damaging the integrity of the country

Note: Paragraph (f) is applicable to Muslim candidate only, a Non-Muslim person is exempted from this criteria.

The Rainbow Effect

June 9th, 2010

Rainbow, the beautiful curve. Some people just watch it, admire the beauty, and walk away. Some people extract a whole philosophy of life out of it.

This Sunday I was out with my family during the rain. After the rain was over, the sweet colorful arc appeared on the blue canvas above. I asked my father to stop the car so I could capture some pictures.

The splendid bow of spectrum that emerged after the rain droplets mesmerized me and the people around. I looked at it, I felt happiness within. I just wanted that moment to become eternal. The tranquility that was gripping my heart, I wanted to remain in that forever. I wanted the time to capture me there.

But as soon as I realized, I saw it fading away. The sun was emerging behind the veils of gray and violet clouds. The beams of sun, as they grew brighter, was slaying the presence of the bow. And soon, it vanished. The same old sun was high, scorching beams were making their ways through the clouds to the grounds. The people around me scattered. They took their ways. For them, probably it was simply a rare beautiful sight.


But it taught me something; the deceiving momentary happiness. Through out our lives, we live with the hope that one day we will get this pure happiness with no sorrows, no rain clouds to darken our atmosphere, no scorching sun to burn the bliss. Just pure happiness, like the rainbow. But we don’t realize, the happiness like that, will fade away. We will be left with the rainfalls or the blazing sun. This is, our real life. And staying in high spirits with this is the real art of living. By following an illusory trail, we can never reach our destination.

Eventually, I started to feel the warmth of sun. The rainbow was gone. I left for my destination.

The Wrath of Poseidon (II)

June 6th, 2010

“I told you so!” said my friend with an eyebrow raised-sarcastic gesture,”the storm wouldn’t hit Karachi, ever!“


This sarcasm was the first thing I had to deal with when the news came about cyclone diverting its path and moving away from Karachi without touching the coast. People who despised my ever so fundamental approach with religion kept telling me such things in a matter-of-fact tone.

“What is the matter? Cat got your tongue?“, said a fellow with tongue in cheek, when I was thinking how to explain to them the meteorological behavior of the storm and the distance and change in speed that resulted in this change of course.

Even if I have to keep the wonderful science aside and talk on the basis of faith, this phenomenon will never get registered to my mind. I have all my respect with saints. They must have been pious people, close to the God, but the whole concept is kind of hard for me to swallow.
